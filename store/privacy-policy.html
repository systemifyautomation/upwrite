Privacy Policy

Product: UpWrite – Upwork Proposal Assistant (Chrome Extension)
Effective date: May 3, 2026
Last updated: May 3, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how UpWrite ("we", "our", or "the extension") handles information when you use the UpWrite Chrome extension. We are committed to protecting your privacy. Please read this policy carefully.

1. What Data UpWrite Accesses

When you use UpWrite on an Upwork proposal page, the extension reads the following information directly from the page in your browser:

  • Job title, job description, client name, and listed budget
  • The current text in your cover letter field and any screening question fields
  • The URL of the proposal page

When you choose to attach a video or screenshot, the extension also accesses:

  • A screen recording or camera recording you initiate
  • A screenshot of the visible tab area
  • A video file you upload from your device
  • A video URL you paste manually

2. How UpWrite Uses Your Data

The data listed above is used exclusively to:

  • Construct a request payload and send it to the webhook URL you configure inside the extension's settings.
  • Display the AI-generated response returned by your webhook inside the extension popup.
  • Auto-fill the proposal form fields on the Upwork page with the AI-generated text.

UpWrite does not send your data anywhere other than the webhook URL you enter. That URL is entirely under your control. We do not operate or have access to any central server that receives proposal data.

3. Data Storage

UpWrite stores the following data locally on your device only:

  • Webhook URL — saved to chrome.storage.sync so it persists across your Chrome sessions.
  • Pending job and AI response — temporarily stored in chrome.storage.local to survive popup close/reopen during proposal generation. Cleared once the proposal is autofilled.
  • Video recordings — stored in the browser's IndexedDB (upwrite-db) on your local device. Never uploaded except to the webhook URL you configure. Deleted when you click "Remove" in the popup or when you record a new video.

No data is stored on any server operated by UpWrite's developers.

4. Data Sharing

UpWrite does not share, sell, rent, or otherwise disclose your data to any third party. The only outbound network request the extension makes is the webhook POST to the URL you configure. The content of that request — and where it goes — is entirely your responsibility and choice.

5. Permissions Justification

The extension requests the following Chrome permissions and uses them as described:

  • activeTab / tabs — Read the URL and content of the active Upwork proposal tab; take a screenshot for AI context.
  • storage / unlimitedStorage — Save your webhook URL (sync) and temporary job/response data (local); store video blobs in IndexedDB without quota limits.
  • scripting — Inject the autofill logic into the Upwork tab after the AI response is received.
  • notifications — Show a completion or error notification when your proposal has been generated.
  • offscreen — Play an audio chime (requires an offscreen document in Manifest V3).
  • alarms — Keep the service worker alive during long webhook requests to prevent Chrome from suspending it mid-request.
  • desktopCapture — Capture screen content for video recording.
  • Host permission — https://www.upwork.com/* — Required to inject the content script that reads the proposal form and fills it with AI-generated text.
  • Host permission — <all_urls> — Required because the webhook endpoint is a URL you configure at runtime (e.g., your own n8n, Make, or custom API server). The extension cannot know this URL at install time, so a wildcard host permission is necessary to allow the fetch. No request is made to any URL other than the one you explicitly enter in settings.

6. Children's Privacy

UpWrite is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any personal information from children.
